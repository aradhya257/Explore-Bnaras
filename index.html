About Banaras

Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the holy river Ganga, it is regarded as the spiritual capital of India and the sacred abode of Lord Shiva — hence the name "Mahadev ki Nagri."

Every evening, thousands gather at the ghats to witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti, a divine ritual of fire, chants, and devotion. Banaras is a living blend of ancient temples, narrow lanes, boat rides at dawn, and timeless spirituality that has drawn seekers and travelers for centuries.

Ghats of Varanasi

84 Total Ghats along the Ganga
(some accounts count up to 88)

Stretching nearly 6.5 kilometres along the western bank of the Ganga, Varanasi's ghats are a continuous chain of stone steps built mostly during the 18th century by Maratha, Rajput and Peshwa patrons. Most ghats are used for bathing and rituals, while Manikarnika Ghat and Harishchandra Ghat are reserved exclusively for cremation.

The five most sacred ghats, called the Panch-Tirtha, are Assi Ghat, Dashashwamedh Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, Panchganga Ghat and Adi Keshav Ghat. A sunrise boat ride from Assi to Manikarnika remains one of the most iconic experiences in India.

Famous Places

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, dedicated to Lord Shiva, standing at the heart of the holy city.

Ganga Ghats

Witness the breathtaking Ganga Aarti and sunrise boat rides along the sacred ghats of Varanasi.

Sarnath

The sacred site where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment.

Sarnath — Buddha's First Sermon

Just 10 km from Varanasi, Sarnath is one of the four holiest Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It was here that Gautama Buddha gave his first teaching, setting the "Wheel of Dharma" in motion after attaining enlightenment at Bodh Gaya.

Dhamek Stupa

A massive cylindrical stupa marking the exact spot where Buddha preached his first sermon to five disciples.

Chaukhandi Stupa

A terraced Gupta-era mound topped with an octagonal Mughal tower, built to mark Buddha's meeting with his disciples.

Ashoka Pillar & Lion Capital

Emperor Ashoka's polished sandstone pillar; its four-lion capital is now India's National Emblem, preserved in Sarnath Museum.

Sarnath Museum & Ruins

The oldest site museum of the Archaeological Survey of India, housing Mauryan, Gupta and Kushan-era Buddhist sculptures.

Sacred Temples of Kashi

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

The most revered of the twelve Jyotirlingas, standing at the very heart of the old city on the banks of the Ganga.

Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple

Founded by saint-poet Tulsidas in the 16th century, this "reliever of troubles" temple is famous for its resident monkeys and its annual music festival.

New Vishwanath Temple (BHU)

Built by the Birla family inside the BHU campus, its 77-metre marble shikhara is the tallest temple tower in the world.

Mansa Devi Mandir

A shakti shrine devoted to Goddess Mansa, worshipped for fulfilling heartfelt wishes and warding off troubles.

Tridev Mandir (Trilochan Mahadev)

An ancient Shivalinga near Trilochan Ghat, revered as the "third eye" of Lord Shiva and mentioned in the Kashi Khand scriptures.

Banaras Hindu University (BHU)

Founded in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, Banaras Hindu University is one of the largest residential universities in Asia and among India's most prestigious institutions, spread over a sprawling 1,300-acre campus on the southern edge of the city.

At its heart stands the New Vishwanath Temple, a marble replica of the Kashi Vishwanath Temple with the world's tallest temple tower. The campus is also home to Bharat Kala Bhavan, a museum of rare miniature paintings and sculptures, and is a favourite hangout for students at its ghat — a short walk from Assi Ghat.

Famous Food of Banaras

Kachori Sabzi

Banaras' iconic breakfast — crisp, lentil-stuffed kachoris served with a spicy potato curry.

Tamatar Chaat

A tangy, spicy tomato-based chaat found only in Varanasi, topped with sev and coriander.

Banarasi Lassi

Thick, creamy lassi topped with malai, served in earthen kulhads — the Blue Lassi shop in Kachori Gali is legendary.

Thandai

A cooling milk drink infused with nuts, saffron and spices — a festive favourite during Holi and Maha Shivratri.

Jalebi & Rabri

Crisp, syrup-soaked jalebis paired with thick sweetened rabri — a beloved Banarasi dessert combo.

Banarasi Paan

Betel leaves filled with areca nut, sweet rose petals and fennel — the perfect way to end a Banarasi meal.

Famous Dress — Banarasi Saree

The Banarasi saree is one of India's finest handwoven silks, famous for its rich gold and silver zari brocade, intricate Mughal-inspired floral motifs, and opulent finish. Woven by generations of artisan families in Varanasi, a single saree can take anywhere from 15 days to several months to complete on a traditional handloom.

Granted a Geographical Indication (GI) tag in 2009, genuine Banarasi silk comes in varieties like Katan (pure silk), Organza, Georgette and Shattir, and remains an essential part of an Indian bride's trousseau to this day.

The Famous Galis of Banaras

With over 1,500 narrow lanes and by-lanes, Varanasi's galis are the living arteries of the old city — connecting ghats, temples, bazaars and centuries-old havelis.

Godowlia & Chowk

The bustling commercial heart of the old city, packed with sweet shops, saree stores and street-food stalls.

Vishwanath Gali

The narrow lane leading straight to Kashi Vishwanath Temple, lined with shops selling flowers, sweets and religious items.

Thatheri Bazaar (Kachori Gali)

Home to century-old havelis, handloom weavers, and the famous Blue Lassi and kachori shops.
